🎯 Pro Tips for Alpine Success

🏔️ Altitude Adaptation

Take first day easy at 2000m+ elevations. Hydrate constantly and watch for altitude sickness symptoms.

⚡ Trail Progression

Start with blues and reds at La Rosière, progress to Les Arcs technical terrain, master La Plagne flow and Tignes jump features.

🕐 Timing Strategy

Hit lifts early (9:00) to avoid crowds. Peak riding hours are 9:00-12:00 and 14:00-16:00.

🔧 Bike Setup

Adjust suspension for Alpine terrain - more sag for technical trails, firmer for jumps and flow.

📸 Content Creation

Best photo spots: Les Arcs ridgeline, La Plagne glacier views, Tignes alpine lakes.

🍕 Fueling Strategy

High-energy breakfast, trail snacks every 2 hours, proper lunch break, post-ride recovery meal.

📝Advanced Planning Notes

  • 🏁 Epic Finale Strategy: Save Les Arcs Black 8 for final day - steep technical drops with spectacular Alpine backdrop
  • ⏰ Critical Time Management: Last lift down by 16:00 to ensure bus connection - negotiate later pickup if extending rides
  • 🎯 Skill Development Focus: Use La Rosière for fundamentals, Paradiski for progression, Tignes for variety
  • 💡 Base Camp Reality: La Rosière chosen by parents - not the most exciting but perfect logistics hub for daily adventures
  • 🌟 Hidden Gems: Early morning rides before lifts open, sunset sessions, off-piste exploration opportunities
